Important Points

  • A stream of electrons moving through a conductor constitutes an electric current. Conventionally, the direction of current is taken opposite to the direction of flow of electrons.The SI unit of electric current is ampere.
  • To set the electrons in motion in an electric circuit, we use a cell or a battery. A cell generates a potential difference across its terminals. It is measured in volts (V).
  • Resistance is a property that resists the flow of electrons in a conductor. It controls the magnitude of the current. The SI unit of resistance is ohm (Ω).
  • Ohm’s law: The potential difference across the ends of a resistor is directly proportional to the current through it, provided its temperature remains the same.
  • A set of resistors connected in parallel has an equivalent resistance Rp given by 1/Rp = 1/R1 + 1/R2 + .....
  • The electrical energy dissipated in a resistor is given by W = V × I × t. The unit of power is watt (W). One watt of power is consumed when 1 A of current flows at a potential difference of 1 V. The commercial unit of electrical energy is kilowatt hour (kWh). 1 kW h = 3,600,000 J = 3.6 × 10 raised to 6 J.
  • The equivalent resistance of several resistors in series is equal to the sum of their individual resistances.
